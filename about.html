About Wikipedia

Shortcuts: WP:ABT · WP:ABOUT · WP:WIKIPEDIA

This is a general introduction for visitors to Wikipedia. For aspiring contributors, see the guide and tutorial. For other uses, see Wikipedia:Wikipedia (disambiguation).

Vision

“Imagine a world in which every single person on the planet is given free access to the sum of all human knowledge. That's what we're doing.” — Jimmy Wales

Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia that anyone can edit, and millions already have. Its purpose is to benefit readers by presenting information on all branches of knowledge.

History and Growth

Created on January 15, 2001, Wikipedia has grown into the world’s largest reference website, attracting over a billion visitors each month. It currently hosts more than 65 million articles in over 300 languages, including 7,042,490 articles in English, with 107,458 active contributors in the past month.

Editing and Principles

Articles are written collaboratively by volunteers known as Wikipedians. Wikipedia’s fundamental principles are summarized in its five pillars. While policies and guidelines exist, new editors don’t need to know them before contributing. Content must be verifiable and neutral. Vandalism and personal opinion are removed quickly by the community.

Anyone can click the Edit or Edit source button (or pencil icon) to improve Wikipedia instantly.

Why Wikipedia is Different

Unlike printed references, Wikipedia is constantly updated. Major news events often have encyclopedic articles within minutes. For over 24 years, editors have built history’s most comprehensive encyclopedia, supporting researchers worldwide.
