From today's featured article

Proceratosaurus is a genus of theropod dinosaur that lived during the Middle Jurassic in what is now England. The holotype and only known specimen consists of a mostly complete skull with a lower jaw and hyoid bone, found near Minchinhampton in Gloucestershire. Originally described as a species of Megalosaurus in 1910, it was moved to its own genus in 1926. The genus name was inspired by its supposed relationship with Ceratosaurus, later shown to be erroneous. Proceratosaurus is now considered one of the oldest known members of the tyrannosauroid lineage, which also includes Tyrannosaurus rex. ( Full article... )

Today's featured picture

The Saint-Gaudens double eagle is a twenty-dollar coin, or double eagle, produced by the United States Mint from 1907 to 1933. The gold coin is named after Augustus Saint-Gaudens, who designed the obverse and reverse. In 1904, President Theodore Roosevelt sought to beautify American coinage, and proposed Saint-Gaudens as an artist capable of the task...
