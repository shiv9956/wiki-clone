Why we have a Universal Code of Conduct

We believe in empowering as many people as possible to actively participate in Wikimedia projects and spaces, to reach our vision of a world in which everyone can share in the sum of all human knowledge...

  • Help create a world in which everyone can freely share in the sum of all knowledge
  • Be part of a global community that will avoid bias and prejudice, and
  • Strive towards accuracy and verifiability in all its work

This Universal Code of Conduct (UCoC) defines a minimum set of guidelines of expected and unacceptable behaviour...

  • Private, public and semi-public interactions
  • Discussions of disagreement and expression of solidarity across community members
  • Issues of technical development
  • Aspects of content contribution
  • Cases of representing affiliates/communities with external partners

1 – Introduction

The Universal Code of Conduct provides a baseline of behaviour for collaboration on Wikimedia projects worldwide...

2 – Expected behaviour

Every Wikimedian, whether they are a new or experienced editor, a community functionary, an affiliate or Wikimedia Foundation board member or employee, is responsible for their own behaviour.

2.1 – Mutual respect

We expect all Wikimedians to show respect for others. In communicating with people, whether in online or offline Wikimedia environments, we will treat each other with mutual respect.

  • Practice empathy. Listen and try to understand...
  • Assume good faith, and engage in constructive edits...
  • Respect the way that contributors name and describe themselves...
  • During in-person meetings, we will be welcoming...

2.2 – Civility, collegiality, mutual support and good citizenship

  • Mentorship and coaching: Helping newcomers...
  • Looking out for fellow contributors...
  • Recognize and credit the work done by contributors...

3 – Unacceptable behaviour

3.1 – Harassment

  • Insults: This includes name calling, using slurs...
  • Sexual harassment: Sexual attention or advances...
  • Threats: Explicitly or implicitly suggesting...
  • Encouraging harm to others...
  • Disclosure of personal data (Doxing)...
  • Hounding: following a person across the project(s)...
  • Trolling: Deliberately disrupting conversations...

3.2 – Abuse of power, privilege, or influence

Abuse occurs when someone in a real or perceived position of power, privilege, or influence engages in disrespectful, cruel, and/or violent behaviour...

3.3 – Content vandalism and abuse of the projects

  • The repeated arbitrary or unmotivated removal of any content...
  • Systematically manipulating content to favour specific interpretations...
  • Hate speech in any form...
  • The use of symbols, images, categories...
